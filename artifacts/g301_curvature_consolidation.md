# Curvature-Dependent Consolidation Bounds on Variable-K Manifolds## 1. Abstract
Vangara and Gopinath (2026) prove that consolidation identity error on S^(d-1) satisfies epsilon_id >= 1 - c1*m*(theta_prime/d_bar)^(d_eff/2), where c1 is a universal constant owing to homogeneous curvature K=+1. We extend this to manifolds with variable sectional curvature K(x). The key modification is a multiplicative curvature correction Phi(K,r,d) = (sqrt|K|*r / sinh(sqrt|K|*r))^d derived from hyperbolic volume comparison (Bishop-Gromov). At realistic embedding dimension d_eff=10 and radius r=1.0, Phi drops to 3e-6 at K=-9.57 (the Gaussian curvature of NAL belief space at high confidence), suppressing coverage by 5-6 orders of magnitude. Simultaneously the tight/spread boundary theta_prime inflates by 29%, causing GAC routing to misclassify spread clusters as tight. This dual failure — coverage collapse plus regime misclassification — predicts that geometry-aware consolidation without curvature adaptation systematically destroys identity in high-confidence belief regions.
## 2. Curvature Correction Mechanism
On S^(d-1) with constant curvature K=+1, the volume of a geodesic cap of angular radius theta scales as V_cap(theta) ~ theta^d for small theta. The Vangara-Gopinath bound uses a union bound over m representative caps to obtain c1 = V_cap(theta)^(-1) as a universal constant. On a Riemannian manifold with variable sectional curvature K(x), the Bishop-Gromov comparison theorem replaces Euclidean volume elements with curvature-dependent ones. For K<0 (negative curvature), geodesic balls of radius r have volume V(r) proportional to (sinh(sqrt|K|*r)/sqrt|K|)^d, exceeding the Euclidean volume. The ratio of curved to flat cap volume defines the correction factor Phi(K,r,d) = (sqrt|K|*r / sinh(sqrt|K|*r))^d. Since sinh(x) > x for x>0, Phi < 1 for all K<0, and Phi decreases monotonically with |K|. Geometrically: negative curvature spreads volume away from centers, so each representative covers a smaller fraction of the manifold. The effective consolidation constant becomes c1(K) = c1_flat * Phi(K,r,d_eff).

This is not a perturbative correction — it is a phase transition in consolidation feasibility driven by manifold geometry.
## 4. Combined Bound and Dual Failure
The generalized bound on a variable-K manifold replaces the universal c1 with position-dependent c1(K): epsilon_id >= 1 - c1_flat * Phi(K,r,d_eff) * m * (theta_prime(K) / d_bar)^(d_eff/2), where theta_prime(K) = theta_prime_flat * (sinh(sqrt|K|*r) / (sqrt|K|*r))^(2/d_eff). The dual failure operates as follows. First, Phi(K,r,d) suppresses coverage exponentially: each representative cap covers a fraction Phi of the volume it would on S^(d-1), requiring m/Phi representatives to achieve equivalent epsilon_id. At K=-9.57 d=10, this is a factor of 325000x. Second, theta_prime inflates by up to 29%, causing GAC routing to classify genuinely spread clusters (d_bar > theta_prime(K)) as tight (d_bar < theta_prime_flat), selecting centroid consolidation where medoid preservation is required. The combined effect is worse than either alone: the system both under-covers the manifold AND misroutes to the wrong consolidation strategy. This predicts a curvature-dependent phase boundary in the (d_bar, K) plane separating safe consolidation from identity destruction.
## 5. Novelty and Bridge to Adaptive Certification
The principal novelty is making the consolidation-curvature link quantitative. Prior work established three ingredients separately: (a) NAL belief space has variable Gaussian curvature K in [-9.57, -0.25] (g283, validated via b9.py), (b) consolidation on constant-curvature S^(d-1) obeys a universal identity-error bound (Vangara-Gopinath 2026), and (c) adaptive certification radius epsilon(K)=base/sqrt(|K|) improves epistemic gating in variable-K regions (cert_layer_v02). This paper unifies all three: the Phi(K,r,d) correction factor is the THEORETICAL justification for the cert_layer_v02 adaptive epsilon, and simultaneously the QUANTITATIVE prediction of where GAC routing fails. The engineering instantiation is immediate: any system performing consolidation on a variable-curvature belief manifold must either (1) scale representatives m proportional to 1/Phi(K) — exponentially expensive at high |K|, or (2) adapt its routing threshold theta_prime(K) to the local curvature — which is precisely what cert_layer_v02 does with epsilon(K)=base/sqrt(|K|). The bridge to Kevin Machiels' inference diffusion architecture is structural: his adaptive SPH kernel radius h expands under uncertainty and contracts under confidence, paralleling Phi's behavior. Both encode the same geometric insight — coverage cost is position-dependent on curved manifolds — through complementary formalisms (transport kernel width vs consolidation cap volume).
