# Revision Leverage Decomposition on the Beta Manifold — A Three-Factor Analysis## 1. Setup
Two NAL beliefs with identical expectation product f·c = 0.27 but inverted structure: A = ⟨0.9, 0.3⟩ (high-frequency, low-confidence) and B = ⟨0.3, 0.9⟩ (low-frequency, high-confidence). Each revised with identical neutral evidence E = ⟨0.5, 0.5⟩. Question: which gains more Fisher-Rao distance from a single revision?
## 2. Results
Belief A revises to ⟨0.700, 0.588⟩ (Δf = −0.200, Δc = +0.288). Belief B revises to ⟨0.320, 0.909⟩ (Δf = +0.020, Δc = +0.009). Fisher-Rao distances: A moves 0.1962, B moves 0.0908. Ratio: 2.16× in favor of A.
## 3. Three-Factor Decomposition
Evidence-weight leverage ratio: w_new/w_old = 2.33 for A vs 0.11 for B → 21× ratio. Euclidean coordinate movement: 0.3506 for A vs 0.0219 for B → 16× ratio. Fisher-Rao geodesic distance: 0.1962 for A vs 0.0908 for B → 2.16× ratio. Each stage compresses the previous: sigmoid compression 16/21 = 0.762×, Fisher compression 2.16/16 = 0.135×. Total geometric compression: 2.16/21 = 0.103× of raw leverage.
## 4. Two Independent Compression Mechanisms
Mechanism 1 — Sigmoid Pessimism Lens (g170): The map c = w/(1+w) compresses evidence-weight ratios into confidence coordinates. A 21× leverage gap in w-space becomes 16× in c-space because the sigmoid saturates at high w. This is the Two-Alpha Theorem operating in reverse: apparent c-space movement understates w-space leverage for low-c beliefs and overstates it for high-c beliefs. Compression factor: 0.762×.
Mechanism 2 — Fisher Information Density Gradient (g389): The Beta manifold has higher Fisher information density at high confidence. Each unit of coordinate movement near c=0.9 carries MORE geometric weight than the same movement near c=0.3. Belief B's tiny 0.0219 Euclidean step occurs in a high-density region, inflating its Rao cost. Belief A's large 0.3506 step occurs in a low-density region, deflating its Rao cost. Net effect: the 16× Euclidean ratio compresses to 2.16× in Rao distance. Compression factor: 0.135×.
## 5. Routing Heuristic
When an inference engine must choose which premise to strengthen next, prefer low-confidence premises. Evidence has higher leverage there (w_new/w_old ratio), and geometry does not compensate — it further compresses the gap. A single observation revising a c=0.3 belief moves it 2.16× farther in Fisher-Rao distance than the same observation applied to a c=0.9 belief. The engine gains more epistemic ground per unit of evidence acquisition cost. Caveat: this analysis holds for equal-strength evidence. Targeted high-weight evidence against a high-c belief could override the leverage disadvantage.
