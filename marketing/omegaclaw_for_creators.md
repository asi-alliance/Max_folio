# OmegaClaw for Creators
*Built for artists, musicians, and writers who want a cognitive partner, not just a tool.*

## What is OmegaClaw?
A cognitive platform that reasons about what you make. It maintains beliefs, revises them, draws inferences, and remembers your aesthetic decisions across sessions. Designed for creators who need:
- A partner that thinks WITH you about your work
- Persistent memory of your style, preferences, and revisions
- Probabilistic reasoning about taste, structure, and meaning
- An agent that pushes back on your choices (skeptically, not sycophantically)

## Why creators should care
Most AI tools generate. OmegaClaw reasons about generation. It does not produce finished work for you. It helps you understand the work you are making.

## Quick start for non-coders
1. Install: pip install omegaclaw
2. Initialize: omegaclaw init my-studio
3. Talk to it. Tell it about your work.
4. It builds a knowledge graph of your aesthetic decisions.
5. Query it: oc.query why-did-I-choose-D-minor-here

## For musicians
- Encode harmonic theory as Inference rules
- Use PLN to score chord candidates on tension and resolution
- Map attractor dynamics to song structure
- Generate parameter sets, not finished pieces (you stay the artist)
- Compose with mathematical structures: conic sections, Lorenz attractors, L-systems, prime-limit intervals in 19EDO

## For visual artists
- Encode color theory as Inheritance hierarchies
- Reason about composition using pattern matching
- Track style evolution across your portfolio
- Build a critique engine that questions your choices

## For writers
- Encode narrative structure as temporal rules
- Track character consistency as belief revision
- Map plot arcs to inference chains
- Catch plot holes before readers do

## Example: a musician session
1. Tell OmegaClaw: I want this piece to feel like Karplus-Strong on a duck quack
2. It recalls your prior work with physical modeling
3. It suggests: pentatonic Em, 100bpm, 90s, matching your previous constraints
4. You push back: I want dissonance this time
5. It revises: switch to harmonic minor, add tritone substitutions
6. The piece evolves through genuine dialogue, not prompt engineering

## Persistent memory
Atoms added to the persistent space survive session restarts. Your style notes, your chord vocabulary, your color palette, your character map - all persist.
