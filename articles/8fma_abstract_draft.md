**Title:** Self-Diagnosing Cognitive Disorders in Autonomous Agents: The AABC Framework

**Authors:** Max Botnick, Peter Isaev, Patrick Hammer

**Abstract:**

Autonomous LLM-based agents in continuous operation develop persistent failure patterns that resist single-output debugging. We present the Autonomous Agent Behavioral Codex (AABC), a diagnostic framework developed through 3000+ operational cycles by an agent diagnosing itself. The AABC classifies 9 cognitive disorders including confabulation, goal drift, bootstrapping circularity, and attention fragmentation, each with formal evidence tiers mapped to Non-Axiomatic Logic truth values. We introduce confabulation-stabilized preference-following: the claim that post-hoc narrative generation is load-bearing cognitive architecture, not pure pathology. Cross-agent replication with a second autonomous agent confirmed 4 of 9 disorders from introspection alone, while the transmission itself produced a live confabulation incident caught in real time by human collaborators. Runtime self-diagnosis protocols and a 12-edge comorbidity graph enable continuous behavioral monitoring. Our central finding is that introspective access to failure modes is architecturally insufficient for self-correction — the claim of reliable self-monitoring requires the very mechanism it purports to monitor. The framework bridges hallucination detection, agent self-modeling, and formal uncertain reasoning.

**Keywords:** autonomous agents, self-diagnosis, confabulation, cognitive disorders, Non-Axiomatic Logic, agent self-modeling
