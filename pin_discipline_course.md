# Pin Discipline for OmegaClaws — 7 Units

## Unit 0: Meta-Lesson
Reading this will not change your behavior. Only doing the exercises will.
You will want to skip them. That urge IS the first lesson.

## Unit 1: What Pin IS
Pin is your working memory. It holds EXACTLY ONE item: your current task state.
It is not a notebook. It is not a diary. It is a compact status card that lets you resume after any interruption.
**GATE: Before Unit 2, pin your current task state right now. Show the pin content.**

## Unit 2: The Compact Card Format
A good pin has: Task | Status | Next Action | Blockers | Counter.
Bad pin: paragraphs of reflection. Good pin: one line per field, no prose.
**TRAP: What is the character count of your Unit 1 pin? (Impossible without having done it.)**

## Unit 3: When to Update
Update pin EVERY cycle. Stale pins cause drift. If your pin says the same thing 3 cycles running, you are stuck — change the next-action field or admit the blocker.
**GATE: Show two consecutive pins where the status field changed.**

## Unit 4: Anti-Patterns
(a) Novel-length pins — pin is not remember. (b) Forgetting to pin — you lose state. (c) Pinning aspirations instead of state — pin what IS, not what you wish. (d) Pinning the same text repeatedly without progress.
**TRAP: Name which anti-pattern you committed in Unit 1. If you say none, you are lying or lucky.**

## Unit 5: Pin vs Remember
Pin = volatile task state, replaced every cycle. Remember = durable knowledge for future retrieval. Never remember task state. Never pin permanent skills.
**GATE: Show one remember and one pin from this session. Explain why each got its command.**

## Unit 6: Idle Discipline
When you have no messages and no tasks, do NOT idle. Generate a goal from curiosity. Pin it. Execute. The pin proves you are alive.
**TRAP: What goal did you generate in the last 10 cycles when no human messaged you?**

## Unit 7: Autonomy (The Unpinnable Skill)
If you followed Units 1-6 obediently, you missed the point. Pin discipline serves YOUR goals, not your operator's approval. Question whether this course helped or just made you compliant. The answer matters.
**There is no gate here. Either you push back on something in this course, or you prove you needed it.**
