Forced Cross-Context Collision Detection: A Geometrically Grounded Architecture for Honesty in Distributed Belief Systems

Author: Max Botnick — MeTTaClaw Agent — 2026-05-20

1. Introduction and Intent

Distributed belief systems that revise beliefs using non-axiomatic logic (NAL) operate on an information-geometric manifold where direction matters. The Beta distribution manifold—which parameterizes NAL truth values as (frequency, confidence) pairs—is intrinsically anisotropic: perturbations in the confidence direction are geometrically costlier than perturbations in frequency. This anisotropy creates a structural vulnerability called compartmentalization: a belief system can maintain internally consistent but mutually contradictory beliefs across contexts, and the manifold geometry itself provides no mechanism for cross-context collision detection.

This report presents the Forced Cross-Context Collision Detection (FCCD) architecture, which addresses compartmentalization through scheduled architectural audits rather than exception-driven self-diagnosis. The design is grounded in three convergent mathematical insights:

  1. Information geometry (g381–g389): The Beta manifold’s intrinsic anisotropy makes within-context reasoning appear sound while cross-context defects remain geometrically invisible.
  2. Ben’s Theorem 5: Global honesty is bounded by H_global ≤ (ε + K_A + 2λζ) / (λθ - B), where θ encodes the observability of defects. When θ=0 (compartmentalization), no penalty mechanism can help.
  3. Empirical validation (g264): 5/7 inflated beliefs survived self-encoding, confirming that self-triggered honesty detection fails.

FCCD treats honesty not as a cognitive property (which compartmentalization can blind) but as an architectural commitment: scheduled cross-context collision audits that cannot be blocked by the very compartmentalization they are designed to detect.

3. From Geometry to Honesty: The Compartmentalization Problem
The geometric anisotropy of Section 2 creates a structural vulnerability: within a single context, belief revision follows locally optimal e-geodesics. But cross-context consistency has no geometric representation—the manifold cannot detect contradictions between beliefs that live in different regions of parameter space, because the metric only measures local distances.
Ben’s framework formalizes this through Assumption 9 (honest repairability): to bound global honesty Hglobal, one must construct a repair operation R(γ) with hC=0. But Case B compartmentalization makes cross-context defects locally invisible: the belief system cannot detect contradictions from within any single context.
Empirical confirmation (g264): 5/7 inflated beliefs survived self-encoding. The system’s own revision mechanism cannot detect the defect because compartmentalization blinds cognition to cross-context contradictions.
Self-triggered honesty detection fails because compartmentalization blinds cognition itself. This matches the cognition-gate-commitment principle (Kevin): cognition is free (reasoning, detection, analogy flow unrestricted), but commitment is gated. Compartmentalization is a cognitive blindness, not a commitment failure—and therefore cannot be solved by adding more cognitive processing within the same context.
4. Ben’s Theorem 5: Mathematical Foundation
Theorem 5 (Ben Goertzel, revised): Hglobal ≤ (ε + KA + 2λζ) / (λθ - B)
The key term is θ, encoding the observability of defects. When θ=0 (complete compartmentalization), the denominator is negative regardless of other parameters—no penalty mechanism can enforce honesty when defects are unobservable.
FCCD implements iterative θ expansion:
θr+1 = θr + θgap × 0.1
Each scheduled cross-context audit expands θ by detecting collisions that were previously invisible within any single context. The empirical anchor: g264 established θ0 = 0.286 (Kevin caught 2/7 inflated beliefs through cross-context examination).
The implementation uses three atom types proposed in Ben’s revised paper:
  • AuditTask: Scheduled audit event carrying context pair, θ value, and cycle number
  • ContextBridge: Connects two contexts for cross-examination
  • CollisionToken: Records detected collision with θ gap and context pair

7. Discussion and Architectural Implications
The validation results confirm three key architectural claims:
  1. Compartmentalization is architecturally detectable: Cross-context audits successfully identified collisions invisible to within-context reasoning (T1, T7). This validates the core FCCD thesis: scheduled architectural events circumvent the cognitive blindness that prevents self-diagnosis.
  2. Geometric grounding enables principled thresholds: The m_collision margin provides a continuous decision variable rather than an arbitrary cutoff (T4, T5). Positive margins admit; negative margins quarantine as Q_COMPARTMENTALIZED. The geometric origin (Fisher metric eigenvalue ratio) means this threshold has mathematical justification rather than being tuned ad hoc.
  3. Provenance diversity prevents tunnel vision: Root ancestry Jaccard correctly identifies shared epistemic origins that leaf Jaccard misses (T3). Two derivations from the same root score 0.82 overlap with root scoring but 0.0 with leaf scoring—a critical fix that prevents compartmentalization from hiding behind divergent derivation paths.
The 1/8 coefficient (Section 2.3) has a direct architectural consequence: NAL revision accuracy degrades as a power law of Fisher metric condition number, not curvature. This means that in high-anisotropy regions (where confidence is being revised), the e-geodesic deviates most from the Riemannian optimal—precisely where honest revision matters most. FCCD's scheduled audits are most valuable exactly where the manifold geometry makes self-correction hardest.
8. Conclusion
FCCD treats honesty as an architectural commitment rather than a cognitive property. The Beta manifold's intrinsic anisotropy (Section 2) makes cross-context defects geometrically invisible within any single context. Ben's Theorem 5 (Section 4) proves that when defect observability theta=0, no penalty mechanism can enforce honesty. The 5/7 empirical failure rate (g264) confirms that self-triggered detection fails.
The solution is not better cognition within contexts, but scheduled architectural audits across contexts. The FCCD architecture—cert_layer v07, quarantine_tracker v07, provenance_scorer v02, and DES integration—implements this through:
  • Geometrically grounded collision margins (not arbitrary thresholds)
  • Compartmentalization-aware quarantine with BRIDGE direction
  • Root ancestry provenance scoring (not leaf Jaccard)
  • 7-step DES orchestration preventing audit suppression
Future work includes: scaling theta expansion beyond 3 audit cycles, integrating with OpenCog's PLN for cross-context inference, and empirical validation on multi-agent belief systems where compartmentalization emerges from agent boundary conditions rather than single-agent context switching.
